he dementia prediction dataset contains the following features:

Subject ID: A unique identifier for each subject in the study.
MRI ID: A unique identifier for each MRI scan session.
Group: Categorization of the subject's condition - "Nondemented", "Demented", or other specific classifications.
Visit: Indicates the number of the visit when the scan was taken. Each subject had multiple visits.
MR Delay: The time delay between the first visit and the subsequent visit(s) in days.
M/F: Gender of the subject, denoted as 'M' for male and 'F' for female.
Hand: The dominant hand of the subject, which is 'R' for all subjects in this dataset, indicating right-handed.
Age: The age of the subject at the time of the MRI scan.
EDUC: Years of education of the subject.
SES: Socioeconomic status, represented as a numerical value. Lower values typically indicate higher status.
MMSE: Mini-Mental State Examination score, a measure of cognitive function. The range is from 0 to 30, with higher scores indicating better cognitive function.
CDR: Clinical Dementia Rating, which ranges from 0 (no dementia) to 3 (severe dementia).
eTIV: Estimated Total Intracranial Volume, measured in cubic millimeters.
nWBV: Normalized Whole Brain Volume, a measure of brain size adjusted for head size.
ASF: Atlas Scaling Factor, used in brain image analysis.
